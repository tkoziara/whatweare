Loneliness and compensation

Jul 16, 2021

The feeling of loneliness is directly related to the sense of separation from Source. The physiology of the human body seems to be cut off from its own nature as the infinite and loving Field of Uniform Consciousness. The human ego, which is the manifestation of the Intelligence of the Field, has a reflex to seek compensation through experiences and stimulation of the definition of its status. There is then complementing of what feels separate by aligning a finite understanding of oneself with a finite perception of the world - grounding oneself in a configuration where the sense of self can be constantly sustained in the stimulation of its definition - and where this fails - resignation, negation, compensation through a domain of instantly accessible sensory experiences that cover and relieve the core loneliness. At the same time, for the human ego, the very possibility of immediate oneness with Source seems to be something inadequate, distant, difficult to relate to in direct experience. An elaborate survival instinct, in the accumulation of beliefs and opinions about itself, keeps itself at a distance from what it is in its Essence. The human ego is a manifestation of a sense of autonomy and independence - it has a hard time admitting that it is really a part of the Intelligence of Divinity that embraces it - fundamentally incapable of securing what it is really seeking - without surrendering.

The human ego is like a rebellious child who, arbitrarily believing that it is unloved, seeks its own independence from an invariably loving parent. In nondual evolution, with the gradual transmutation of the impressions that express this "arbitrary faith", human physiology finds itself returning into the loving embrace of Source. What remains of the human ego - the lens of individuality powered by residual impressions - in the effortlessly evident nature of itself as the Unity of the Field, ceases to seek fulfillment in the realm of experience. Devotion to the Nonduality of Self becomes a natural reflex. The flow of Love towards Source is like an echo - in expressing and directing Love into the depth of Self - Love returns in twofold. In Nonduality, Point and Wholeness are like a child and parent - the embrace of the Field of Consciousness - the arms of Source - is the embrace of Self.

The deeper the perspective shifts in the evolution towards Source - the more darkness, resistance, and denial of the Truth of Nonduality are encountered in the residual structure of the human ego and impressions that are powered in a greater degree by the collective material of the sense of separation supported by forms of lower astral activity. I am writing about this because I do not want to sketch an idealized form of nondual evolution. Perspective can rest in a clear recognition of the Oneness of Self, and at the same time the space of transmutation can contain impressions and forms of identification that express a fierce rebellion against one's own nature as Divinity. During periods of clarity, the flow of devotional Love may be pure - in itself in the value of the Field or in relation to a symbol of Divinity that is seen as an aspect of the Field/Self. But just as often, transmutation can encounter forms of Divine Intelligence that actively oppose their own Divinity - as part of the building blocks of the collective sense of separation and use insight into the nature of impressions at the base of incarnation to disrupt the process of nondual evolution. There may be doubts or a desire to stop at what has already been realized in the perspective of the body. There may be an aversion to the very idea of the fullness evolution in the realization of Pure Divinity - even if This Perspective has already tasted ItSelf through the point of the body - it may seem that the current Nondual Modality is sufficient to live the rest of human life. In the visibility of the illusion of reality that builds the human ego - and in the visibility of the overwhelming proportion of this illusion in the human world - it may seem that further evolution does not matter. There may be reflexes to concede - to conform to aspects of the interdependence of the human ego as "something that can be left as it is" - without the will to further transcend and transmute them. Nevertheless, regardless of all this, the point of the body remains in the dynamics of evolution towards the realization of Pure Divinity of Self.
